Sugarcane

Overview

Sugarcane is a map generation framework and runtime environment for Minecraft. Its raison d'être is primarily focussed on the generation of graphical maps of Minecraft worlds, but it could potentially be used for any kind of analysis/processing of world data.

The major design goals for Sugarcane is that it must be extensible, and support efficient use of distributed computational resources.

Extensibility

All aspects of Sugarcane rely on a plugin system, allowing alternate implementations of various functionality to be swapped in and out at runtime, without having to rebuild Sugarcane itself. This makes it easy for third parties to extend Sugarcane's capabilities, and simple for end-users to make use of.

Distribution

Processing Minecraft worlds can frequently be computationally expensive, especially for large worlds. Thus, Sugarcane is designed to support not only vertical scaling, but also allow for horizontal scaling. It is important to note, however, that Sugarcane still fully supports a single-host configuration.
